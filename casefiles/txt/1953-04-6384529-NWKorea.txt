AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

SUBJECT: Observation of an Unidentified Object
AREA REPORTED ON: Northwest Korea
FROM: Intelligence Office, 3d Bombardment Wing (L), APO 270
DATE OF REPORT: 4 April 1953    DATE OF INFORMATION: 3 April 1953    EVALUATION: B-1
PREPARED BY: Kermit W. Fish, Captain, USAF, 3d Bombardment Group Intelligence
REFERENCES: APL 200-5; 3d Bomb Group Mission #3-26909, dated 2/3 April 1953.

SUMMARY: 3 April 1953, at 0225Z, in the vicinity of UTM coordinates TQ-3050, Lt. Colonel John D. Dillon, a B-25 pilot flying night armed reconnaissance on a heading of 355 degrees, with an indicated speed of 200 mph, and at an altitude of 5,000 feet, 500 feet on top of a solid overcast, observed a bluish-green object about 200 feet from his aircraft, on a level, parallel flight with the aircraft. The heading of the object was the same as that of the aircraft. The object turned from bluish-green to red and then turned red and disappeared. He observed the object for approximately ten (10) seconds.

2. 1/Lt Perry observed a white light descending from very high altitude to approximately level with his aircraft. The object was traveling at a very high speed on a heading of approximately 100 degrees. 1/Lt Perry would not attempt to estimate the distance of the object from the aircraft. The object appeared to be white with no noticeable change in color. He estimated that it was observed for approximately five (5) seconds.

3. 2/Lt Vermillion's statements were the same as the above with the exception that the color of the object was bluish at first, then turned green, then turned red and disappeared. He observed the object for approximately ten (10) seconds.

4. A/2C Caruthers stated the same as above although he observed the object to be green at first, then turned red and then white and went out. He stated these changes of color were simultaneous. He estimated his observation to be approximately three (3) seconds. He observed this object from a 35 to 40 degree angle above the aircraft, and falling at a very rapid speed. He stated it resembled a meteor, and he estimated the size to be approximately five (5) feet in width.

COMMENTS OF THE PREPARING OFFICER: This crew was very cooperative in reporting this object. The crew members have completed the following number of combat missions: 1/Lt Perry, 65 missions; 2/Lt Vermillion, 70 missions; A/2C Caruthers, 12 missions. The information given by this crew is considered to be reliable.

COMMENT OF THE APPROVING OFFICER: It is the opinion of this Directorate that this was probably the sighting of an unusually large meteor. At the time of this sighting the moon was entering the third quarter, which might have prevented the object from dissipating to large extent; thus being readily observed. The reported change in color could have been due to: 1) the composition of the meteor; 2) the refraction caused by different height angles of observation through the atmosphere; and 3) the effect of the color of the cockpit and navigation/explosion lights in the viewer's eyes.

This Directorate has no previous report of a similar observation on file. There was no evidence to indicate that the object was controlled in any manner, or that it was anything but an unusually large meteor. (Meteor)

COMMENT of the Approving Officer:

Information at this Directorate indicates that this was a possible observation of a small meteor. The changing color and sparks are probably the result of the meteor burning out in the earth's atmosphere. Because of the difficulty of estimating distance at night, the 200 feet may have been much more. It is doubted that this object was a projectile fired from an aircraft or ground weapon, or another aircraft.
