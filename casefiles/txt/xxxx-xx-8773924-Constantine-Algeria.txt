REF CASE - 16 JUL 1952,
RABAT-FR MOR,
LINCL
00-W-24639

At 2115 hours on 14 August 1952, two persons in the city of Constantine saw a luminous object flying at high speed toward Guelma. According to the witnesses, the apparatus, which had no resemblance to a flying saucer, emitted a very bright light. At the time this happened, the local weather bureau had not launched any weather balloons.

The same day, at 1920 hours, many people at the docks of Philippeville saw in the sky an enormous red disk going from north to west, leaving behind a greenish trail.

TANGIER, ESPANA                         12 Aug 52

SAUCER-LIKE CRAFT SEEN IN SPAIN--Tangier, Espana, 12 Aug 52

According to the Cifra news agency, on the evening of 10 August 1952, a resident of Cuenca, Spain, while walking along the Cuenca-Madrid road, saw an object similar to the so-called flying saucers. It crossed the sky at high speed in a matter of seconds. The object was round and gave off two luminous trails.

[handwritten: Hydrographic Bulletin]

METEORS

The Hydrographic Office is cooperating with astronomers who are studying meteors. Mariners reporting their observations of these dies are greatly assisting this work. It is desired to have the Greenwich times and point of appearance and disappearance as accurate as possible, either by bearing and altitude, or by relation to fixed stars, or both.
Complete observations on long-enduring trains and their direction of drift are of especial importance, as they determine wind direction in the upper atmosphere.

NORTH PACIFIC

Mr. G. A. Linnartz, an officer aboard the American S. S. Norman Lykes, Capt. H. Johnson, Master, reports that at 1725 G. M. T., August 12, 1952, in lat. 30°03' N., lon. 154°18' E., he observed a brilliant meteor. It appeared near Deneb at 30° altitude bearing 310° and arced toward the horizon and disappeared at about 5° altitude directly below Polaris. It was of about 5 seconds' duration and of such brilliance as to light up the entire area. It left a rather short tail from which pieces appeared to be falling. Several smaller meteors were seen both before and after this brilliant meteor.
Weather partly cloudy, barometer 29.94 inches, air temperature 81° F., sea temperature 83° F.
