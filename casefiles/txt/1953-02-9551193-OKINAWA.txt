INVESTIGATION REPORT
Subject: Unidentified Flying Object — Okinawa, 7 February 1953

I. Description of Sighting:

Radar at [unit] tracked unidentified object for approximately 15 minutes. Interceptor alerted and F-94 scrambled. Aircraft climbed to 15,000 feet. Visual contact established — object appeared bright orange, changing to intermingled red and green colors. F-94 attempted stern chase; object pulled away at high speed. F-94 broke off pursuit due to fuel shortage. No physical evidence.

II. Discussion:

Weather at time of sighting: scattered stratus clouds.

Venus: At time of sighting, Venus was located at azimuth 273°, elevation approximately 10° above horizon. Venus is extremely bright during this period of the year and is readily visible to both ground and airborne observers.

Radar analysis: Radar plot probably represents spurious/false target. No strict correlation between electronic plots and visual contact established. F-94 was vectored toward Venus position.

Mars: Also visible in same general area of sky.

There is no strict correlation between the electronics and the visual. The F-94 was vectored to the position of Venus. The intermingled red and green colors noted by the F-94 crew are consistent with Venus observed through varying atmospheric layers at low elevation angle.

III. Conclusion:

The visual sighting is assessed as Probably Venus. The radar plots are assessed as False Targets (spurious returns).
